History

First explored in 1516 by Juan Diaz de Solis, Argentina developed slowly under Spanish colonial rule. Argentinians set up their own government in 1810. On July 9, 1816, independence was formally declared from Spanish rule.

Culture

Argentina is considered one of the melting pots of Latin America. With a sizable population of Italian, French, German, and even Japanese decent. The official language of Argentina is Spanish, but many languages can be spoken such as German, Italian, and other European languages.
